My earliest professional engineering work was as an **Android Engineer at Shanghai Jinglian
Information and Technology**, building **mobile insurance solutions** — specifically, apps
that helped assess vehicle damage in the field. It's where I learned how to ship real mobile
software to real users on a sprint cadence.

## What it did

The core product was a **damage-assessment app** for insurance workflows. Adjusters in the
field could capture and process damage information on-site, with **location and mapping
powered by the Baidu Maps APIs** and a **custom Android UI** built around the assessment
flow.

## What I'm proud of

Two things from this role stuck with me:

- **Performance work that mattered.** I **refactored core algorithms and cut memory usage by
  25%** — the kind of unglamorous optimization that, on the constrained Android devices of
  the time, was the difference between an app that felt smooth and one that didn't.
- **Shipping discipline.** I delivered **sprint-based product updates** with proper version
  control (**Git/SVN**) — getting the habits of iterative, releasable software early.

## Why it still matters to me

This was the foundation: real users, real constraints, real deadlines. The instinct to
**measure first, then optimize the thing that actually hurts** — rather than guessing — is
exactly the mindset I carry into data and analytics work today. Performance, like analytics,
rewards looking at the numbers before you act.
